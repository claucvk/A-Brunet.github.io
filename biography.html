Biography

About me

I am Annael Brunet, in the 3rd year of a PhD in physics in both the theoretical Physics Laboratory under the supervision of Nicolas Destainville and the Institute of Pharmacology and Structural Biology under the supervision of Catherine Tardin (Both labs are in Toulouse, South of France). My research is focused on the investigating the impact of intrinsic bending, local denaturation, or by a global effect due to variation of concentration of the surrounding mobiles ions in solution on conformation states of DNA molecules. The study consiste in the development and propose a joint theory-experiment high-hroughput approach, corresponding to manage in parallel experimental technics, numerical simulations and, physics models.

About

An experimental and theoretical study at the single molecule level of conformational changes of the DNA molecule. I am investigate the impact of local defects included in the DNA molecule and of physicochemical conditions set by the surrounding mobiles ions presente in the solvent.

My will is to continue in the condensed matter field at the frontier with biology. Besides, I am extremely eager to explore the conformations of the DNA in its native state such as it is organized in chromatin. I am very interested by the research on the genome architecture and the dynamic of chromosome folding.
